# Spanish Greetings and Introductions

## Question

What are the common Spanish greetings and introductions, and when should you use each one?

## Answer

### Formal Greetings

| Spanish | English | Usage |
|---------|---------|-------|
| Buenos días | Good morning | Used until noon |
| Buenas tardes | Good afternoon | Used from noon until sunset |
| Buenas noches | Good evening/night | Used after sunset |
| Hola, mucho gusto | Hello, nice to meet you | Formal introduction |
| ¿Cómo está usted? | How are you? | Formal "you" form |
| Encantado/a de conocerle | Pleased to meet you | Formal introduction (masculine/feminine forms) |

### Informal Greetings

| Spanish | English | Usage |
|---------|---------|-------|
| ¡Hola! | Hello! | Universal informal greeting |
| ¿Qué tal? | How's it going? | Casual greeting |
| ¿Cómo estás? | How are you? | Informal "you" form |
| ¿Qué pasa? | What's happening? | Very casual, among friends |
| ¿Qué onda? | What's up? | Slang, used in Latin America |
| ¡Buenas! | Hi there! | Shortened form of formal greetings, slightly informal |

### Regional Variations

- **Spain**: "Hola, ¿qué tal?" is very common
- **Mexico**: "¿Qué onda?" or "¿Qué hubo?" (What's up?)
- **Argentina**: "¡Che, boludo!" (among close male friends) or "¿Cómo andás?" (How are you doing?)
- **Colombia**: "¿Quiubo?" (Contraction of "¿Qué hubo?") or "¿Cómo vas?"
- **Caribbean**: "¿Dimelo?" (Tell me) in Dominican Republic

### Introductions

**Introducing yourself:**

- "Me llamo..." (My name is...)
- "Soy..." (I am...)

**Asking someone's name:**

- "¿Cómo te llamas?" (informal)
- "¿Cómo se llama usted?" (formal)

**Saying where you're from:**

- "Soy de..." (I'm from...)
- "Vengo de..." (I come from...)

### Time-based Considerations

In Spanish-speaking countries, greetings often follow the time of day more strictly than in English:

- Use "Buenos días" typically until 12:00 PM
- Switch to "Buenas tardes" from around 12:00 PM until sunset (6-8 PM)
- Use "Buenas noches" after sunset both as a greeting and when saying goodbye

### Cultural Note

Physical greetings vary by country:

- One kiss on the cheek is common in many countries (between women or a man and woman)
- Two kisses (one on each cheek) in Spain
- Handshakes in more formal settings
- Hugs (abrazos) between close friends
